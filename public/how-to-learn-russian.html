How to Learn Russian for Free

There's no perfect method — but there is a sensible order. This is a realistic, opinionated roadmap built around the resources in our directory.

Phase 1 — Cyrillic (Week 1)

Don't skip this. Spend a focused week drilling the alphabet and basic pronunciation. Use the free Cyrillic quiz and resources from Alphabet & Phonetics.

Phase 2 — Survival basics (Weeks 2–6)

Greetings, numbers, common verbs, present tense, basic question words. Browse Getting Started and Courses & MOOCs.

Phase 3 — Cases & aspect (Months 2–6)

This is the long climb. Read our Russian Grammar guide and pick a coherent course. Don't grammar-hop.

Phase 4 — Input, input, input (Months 6+)

Massive comprehensible input through podcasts, YouTube, and books. Speak with humans via language exchange.

The secret ingredient: consistency

Daily practice — even 10 minutes — outperforms weekend marathons. Our Daily Russian Practice gives you the same 10 questions as everyone else, every day. Build a streak. Beat your friends.
