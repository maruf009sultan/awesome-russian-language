📝 Grammar

The Free Guide to Russian Grammar

Russian grammar has a reputation — and yes, the cases are real — but it's also one of the most logical, pattern-rich systems in any major language. Here's where to start.

The 6 Russian cases

Russian uses six grammatical cases — Nominative, Genitive, Dative, Accusative, Instrumental, and Prepositional — to mark a word's role in a sentence. English uses word order to do the same job; Russian uses endings. Once the case system clicks, sentences become surprisingly flexible.

Verbal aspect: perfective and imperfective

Every Russian verb comes in two flavors — imperfective (ongoing, repeated, unfinished) and perfective (completed, one-time). It feels alien to English speakers but is far more regular than English tense. Spend serious time here; it pays off.
