📖 Beginner's Guide

Learn the Russian Cyrillic Alphabet

The Cyrillic alphabet looks intimidating at first — but with the right resources, most learners read full Russian words within a week. This is your free, no-signup starting point.

Why start with the alphabet?

Russian uses the Cyrillic script, which has 33 letters: 10 vowels, 21 consonants, and 2 signs (the hard sign Ъ and the soft sign Ь). Several letters look like English letters but sound completely different — В is "v", Н is "n", Р is a rolled "r", and Х is the "kh" in Bach. Mastering the alphabet first means every other Russian resource — textbooks, podcasts, Anki decks — becomes accessible.

The fastest path

  • Spend 2–3 days drilling letter shapes and sounds with our free Cyrillic quiz.
  • Read short cognates ("ресторан", "телефон", "музыка") to build confidence.
  • Practice writing each letter by hand — muscle memory makes recognition automatic.
  • Move on to grammar and vocabulary only once reading feels effortless.
